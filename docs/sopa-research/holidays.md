Statewide public holidays only. Excludes regional/local holidays and the NSW
"Bank Holiday" (applies to banks/ADIs only, not a general public holiday).
SA part-day holidays (24 Dec and 31 Dec, 7pm–midnight) are listed separately.

## New South Wales

Source of law: Public Holidays Act 2010 (NSW). Easter Sunday has been a NSW
public holiday since 2011 — confirmed on the official list below. Anzac Day is
observed on 25 April with NO substitute/additional day when it falls on a
weekend. Additional days apply only when New Year's Day, Christmas Day or
Boxing Day fall on a weekend (the weekend day remains a holiday and an extra
day is added).

## Victoria

Source of law: Public Holidays Act 1993 (Vic). Victoria observes both
"Saturday before Easter Sunday" (Easter Saturday) and Easter Sunday. Friday
before the AFL Grand Final is a statewide public holiday (since 2015); its
date depends on the AFL fixture. Melbourne Cup Day (first Tuesday in November)
is statewide unless a non-metro council has arranged an alternate local
holiday. Anzac Day has no replacement when it falls on a weekend.

## South Australia

Source of law: Public Holidays Act 2023 (SA), in force 1 Jan 2024 (replaced
the Holidays Act 1910). Easter Sunday IS a statewide SA public holiday since
1 Jan 2024 — all four Easter days are public holidays. SA observes
Proclamation Day (in lieu of Boxing Day) on 26 December. Weekend rules per
SafeWork SA:
- 25 Dec / 1 Jan on a Sat or Sun: that day AND the following Monday are both
  public holidays.
- 26 Dec (Proclamation Day) on a Sat: that day and the following Monday; on a
  Sun or Mon: that day and the following Tuesday.
- 26 Jan (Australia Day) on a Sat/Sun: the following Monday is the holiday
  INSTEAD of that day.
- 25 Apr (Anzac Day): no substitute or additional day when on a weekend.
