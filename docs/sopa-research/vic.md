# Victorian Security of Payment — Research for Sopal (current as at 10 June 2026)

> **HEADLINE: Victoria's SOPA regime was fundamentally rewritten with effect from 15 April 2026.**
> The *Building Legislation Amendment (Fairer Payments on Jobsites and Other Matters) Act 2025* (Vic) (No. 43/2025) received Royal Assent on **13 November 2025** and its SOP Act amendments (Part 2, almost in full) **commenced on 15 April 2026** by proclamation (Special Gazette (No. 189), 14 April 2026, p. 1). The current authorised consolidation is **Version 014, incorporating amendments as at 15 April 2026** — this document is based on that consolidation, downloaded from legislation.vic.gov.au.
>
> As at June 2026 the **operative regime** is the NEW regime: reference dates abolished (monthly claim entitlement), excluded amounts / claimable variations (ss 10A–10B) **repealed**, 6-month outer limit for claims, 20-business-day statutory payment cap, performance security claims/notice regime, ban on new reasons in adjudication responses, notice-based time bar unfairness (s 13A), review adjudication abolished, new business day definition (22 Dec – 10 Jan shutdown).
>
> **Uncommenced (imminent)**: only two provisions of the amending Act did not commence on 15 April 2026 — **s 25(4)** (inserts s 23(2)(da): adjudicator must consider the performance security schedule and claimant submissions on a performance security claim) and **s 45(3)** (substitutes s 45(5): adjudicator forfeits fees for late determination, updated cross-references). They commence on proclamation or, by forced commencement under s 2(3) of the amending Act, on **1 September 2026**.
>
> **Transitional (s 54)**: the amended Act applies to contracts entered **before, on or after** 15 April 2026 (retrospective to existing contracts). BUT the Part 3 amendments do **not** apply to a payment claim served under s 14, or an adjudication application made under s 18 but not determined, **before 15 April 2026** — those tail cases continue under the old regime (Version 013, summarised below where relevant). Exceptions: new s 28R (enforcement), s 28Q (certificates), s 28 (new application), and s 50 (service) apply to old claims too — see s 54(3), (7), (8), (9).

## 1. Act metadata

- **Full title**: *Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 2002* (Vic), No. 15 of 2002. Assented to 14 May 2002; commenced 31 January 2003.
- **Citation form**: "s 14 of the *Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 2002* (Vic)"; short form used in practice: "s 14 SOP Act (Vic)". Note the singular "Payment" in the title (commonly mis-cited as "Payments").
- **Current consolidation**: Version 014, incorporating amendments as at **15 April 2026** (authorised PDF/Word on legislation.vic.gov.au).
- **Amending Act**: *Building Legislation Amendment (Fairer Payments on Jobsites and Other Matters) Act 2025* (Vic), No. 43/2025. Assent 13.11.2025. SOP Act amendments (ss 3–25(3), 25(5)–45(2), 45(4)–54) commenced **15.4.2026**; ss 25(4) and 45(3) commence by proclamation, forced **1 September 2026**.
- **Regulations**: *Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Regulations 2023* (S.R. No. 34/2023), Version 002 as at **3 June 2026** (amended by the *Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Amendment Regulations 2026*, S.R. No. 60/2026, made 2.6.26, commenced 3.6.26). The Regulations prescribe **forms only for**: notice of intention to exercise lien (s 12A), notice of claim (s 32), debt certificate (s 33), discharge notice (s 40), s 41 notice — there is **no prescribed form for payment claims, payment schedules, performance security claims/schedules or adjudication documents**. New reg 11 (from 3 June 2026) prescribes **e-lockbox upload** as a manner of service on authorised nominating authorities (service effected when uploaded and retrievable). Regulations sunset 23 May 2033.
- **Administering body**: Victorian Building Authority ("the Authority") — note the VBA is transitioning into the Building and Plumbing Commission under separate reforms; the Act defines "Authority" as the VBA established under the Building Act 1993.
- **Adjudication model**: claimant chooses a private **authorised nominating authority (ANA)** (s 18(3)(b)) — unlike QLD's registrar model.

### Key definitions (s 4(1), current)

- **business day** (amended by No. 43/2025 s. 5(6)) — verbatim:
  > "**business day** means a day that is not—
  > (a) a Saturday or Sunday; or
  > (b) a day that is wholly or partly observed as a public holiday throughout Victoria; or
  > (c) a day during the period commencing on 22 December in any year and ending on 10 January in the following year;"

  This is the single most important change for a due-date calculator: the **22 Dec – 10 Jan industry shutdown** (inclusive) now stops all business-day clocks, and "wholly or partly observed" public holidays **throughout Victoria** are excluded (statewide holidays only, not regional ones).
- **named month** — "means January, February, March, April, May, June, July, August, September, October, November or December". (Replaces reference dates as the trigger concept.)
- **progress payment** (substituted by 43/2025) — a payment that **includes** (a) the final payment; (b) a single or one-off payment; (c) "a payment that is based on an event or date (known in the building and construction industry as a \"milestone payment\")".
- **reference date** — **GONE**. The s 9(2) reference date definition was removed when s 9 was substituted by No. 43/2025 s. 7. New s 9(1): "A person is entitled under this Act to a progress payment under a construction contract if the person has undertaken to carry out construction work or to supply related goods and services under the contract."
- **claimable variation** — def. **repealed** by No. 43/2025 s. 5(2). Section 10A **repealed** by No. 43/2025 s. 9.
- **excluded amount** — def. **repealed** by No. 43/2025 s. 5(2). Section 10B **repealed** by No. 43/2025 s. 9.
- **practical completion** — new def.: the day provided in the contract, or otherwise the day the work is completed as required under the contract and there are no omissions/defects that materially prevent the intended use. (Drives the 6-month claim limit in s 14C.)
- **performance security** = performance bond (guarantee/bond) or retention money (both defined, GST-inclusive for retention).
- **defects liability period** — starts on practical completion day, ends when rectification work as required/directed is completed.
- **due date** — (a) day a progress payment becomes due and payable under s 12(1); (b) day a performance security is due to be released under s 12(1A).
- **claimant / respondent / payment claim / payment schedule / claimed amount / scheduled amount** — as per ss 14, 15 (payment schedule now includes a second-chance schedule under s 18(2A)).
- **Application exclusions (s 7)** unchanged: domestic building contracts with owner-occupiers (s 7(2)(b)–(ba)), non-value-based consideration contracts (s 7(2)(c)), lending/guarantee arrangements, work outside Victoria, employees.
- **No contracting out**: s 48.

## 2. Payment claims (for Payment Claim Reviewer)

### s 14 validity requirements (current, post-15 April 2026)

s 14(1): a person referred to in s 9(1) who is or claims to be entitled to a progress payment may serve a payment claim on the person who, under the construction contract, "is or may be liable to make the payment."

s 14(2) — verbatim:
> "(2) A payment claim—
> (a) must be in the relevant prescribed form (if any); and
> (b) must contain the prescribed information (if any); and
> (c) must identify the construction work or related goods and services to which the progress payment relates; and
> (d) must indicate the amount of the progress payment that the claimant claims to be due (the claimed amount); and
> (e) must state that it is made under this Act."

- No form or information is currently prescribed (Regulations 2023 v002 prescribe none for payment claims), so the operative requirements are (c), (d), (e).
- **The "must not include any excluded amount" requirement (old s 14(3)(b)) is GONE.** New s 14(3): "The claimed amount may include any amount that the other person is liable to pay the claimant under section 29(4)" (suspension loss/expense). Old s 14(4)–(9) (3-month limit, reference-date and one-claim-per-reference-date rules, final/single/one-off restrictions) repealed by No. 43/2025 s. 15(4).
- Claims can now include **variations of any kind, delay and other time-related costs, latent conditions, changes in regulatory requirements, and damages claims** — the entire excluded-amounts gate is removed for claims served on/after 15 April 2026.

### Timing — earliest (s 14A, new)

s 14A(1): a payment claim may be served "on and from the last day of— (i) the named month in which the construction work was first carried out under a construction contract; and (ii) each subsequent named month during which any further construction work is carried out under that contract" (mirrored for related goods and services in s 14A(1)(b)).

- **December rules** (s 14A(2)): work done **1–21 December** → claim may be served on and from **22 December** of that year; work done **22–31 December** → claim may be served on and from **31 January** the following year.
- Contract may permit **earlier** service (s 14A(3)) but a contract term making the earliest service day **later** than the last day of the month has **no effect** (s 14B(1)(a)) — except a contract may defer claims for 22–31 Dec work to 31 January (s 14B(2)). Milestone-payment claims cannot be restricted to less frequently than once a month (s 14B(1)(b)).
- **Termination**: on service of a notice of termination or termination by agreement, a claim may be served on and from the contractual termination day (s 14A(4)).
- **Premature service is cured**: s 14A(5) — a claim served early "is not invalid", is "taken to be served on the earliest day", and the payment-schedule clock "does not commence until the earliest day".
- Entitlement to serve survives termination/expiry of the contract (s 14A(6)).

### Timing — latest (s 14C, new)

Verbatim:
> "A payment claim may be served no later than the day before the latest of the following—
> (a) the date (if any) determined under the terms of the construction contract as the latest day on which the payment claim may be served;
> (b) the date that is 6 months after the practical completion of all construction work to be carried out under the construction contract;
> (c) the date that is 6 months after the supply of all related goods and services to be supplied under the construction contract."

i.e. **6 months after practical completion** (up from 3 months after the reference date), or a longer contractual period. Note the words "the day before the latest of" — service must occur before (not on) the latest date.

### Frequency (s 14D, new)

- Default: **one payment claim per named month** (s 14D(1)), unless the contract allows more.
- s 14D(2) expressly permits: a single claim covering more than one progress payment; **re-including previously claimed unpaid amounts**; and claiming in a later month for work done in an earlier month.

### Final / single / one-off payments

- All three are within the definition of "progress payment" (s 4(1) def., paras (a)–(b)), as are milestone payments (para (c)). The old special service windows and the old "only one final/single/one-off claim ever" rule (old s 14(5)–(7)) were repealed — final/one-off claims now ride on the same s 14A–14D machinery (monthly entitlement + s 14C outer limit).

### Excluded amounts (s 10B) and claimable variations (s 10A) — status

- **REPEALED in their entirety on 15 April 2026** (No. 43/2025 s. 9). The 10%/$5M "second class" variation thresholds are gone. Valuation under s 11(1)(b)(iii)/(2)(b)(iii) now simply refers to "any variation agreed to by the parties to the contract by which the contract price … is to be adjusted".
- **Legacy (claims served before 15 April 2026 — still governed by old law via s 54(2))**, from Version 013:
  - Old s 10(3): "an excluded amount must not be taken into account in calculating the amount of a progress payment".
  - Old s 10B(2) — verbatim:
    > "(2) The excluded amounts are—
    > (a) any amount that relates to a variation of the construction contract that is not a claimable variation;
    > (b) any amount (other than a claimable variation) claimed under the construction contract for compensation due to the happening of an event including any amount relating to—
    > (i) latent conditions; and
    > (ii) time-related costs; and
    > (iii) changes in regulatory requirements;
    > (c) any amount claimed for damages for breach of the construction contract or for any other claim for damages arising under or in connection with the contract;
    > (d) any amount in relation to a claim arising at law other than under the construction contract;
    > (e) any amount of a class prescribed by the regulations as an excluded amount."
  - Old s 10A classes: **first class** = fully agreed variations (agreement on scope, entitlement, value/method, time for payment). **Second class** = directed/requested but disputed variations, claimable only if (old s 10A(3)(d)) the contract consideration at contract date "(i) is $5 000 000 or less; or (ii) exceeds $5 000 000 but the contract does not provide a method of resolving disputes". Old s 10A(4): once total disputed-variation claims exceed **10% of the contract sum**, the $5M figure reads as **$150 000** (so disputed variations under a contract over $150k with a dispute clause cease to be claimable).
  - Old s 14(3)(b): the claimed amount "must not include any excluded amount"; old s 16(4)(a)(ii) barred court judgment (no-schedule route) unless the claim contained no excluded amount (*Yuanda*).
  - Old s 14(4)–(5): claims could be served within the contract period or **3 months after the relevant reference date**, whichever later (final/single/one-off: contract period, or 3 months after the reference date).
  - Old s 9(2) reference dates (default scheme, contract silent): (b) progress — **20 business days** after the previous reference date (first: 20 BD after work first carried out); (c) single/one-off — the date immediately following the day work was last carried out; (d) final — the date immediately following expiry of any defects rectification period, or issue of a final certificate, or otherwise the day work was last carried out.

### Key case law — payment claims

All decided under the pre-15 April 2026 regime; holdings on identification/service/jurisdiction remain good law, excluded-amounts holdings are now historical (but still apply to legacy claims).

1. **Protectavale Pty Ltd v K2K Pty Ltd [2008] FCA 1248** (Finkelstein J) — objective test for s 14(2)(c): a claim must identify the work and amount sufficiently for a reasonable recipient, in the parties' factual context, to understand and respond to it; a confusing final-account invoice failed.
2. **Hickory Developments Pty Ltd v Schiavello (Vic) Pty Ltd [2009] VSC 156** (Vickery J) — a valid s 14 payment claim is a "basic and essential" precondition to a valid adjudication; identification requirements applied with a practical, non-pedantic eye.
3. **Grocon Constructors Pty Ltd v Planit Cocciardi Joint Venture (No 2) [2009] VSC 426** (Vickery J) — Victorian adjudication determinations are reviewable for jurisdictional error (certiorari); Victoria diverged from the narrow NSW Brodyn approach.
4. **Gantley Pty Ltd v Phoenix International Group Pty Ltd [2010] VSC 106** (Vickery J) — payment claim must sufficiently identify the work; portions of a claim comprising excluded amounts could be severed at adjudication (old regime).
5. **Metacorp Australia Pty Ltd v Andeco Construction Group Pty Ltd [2010] VSC 199** (Vickery J) — service and timing requirements analysed; valid service is a jurisdictional fact, but the Act is to be applied without excessive technicality.
6. **Seabay Properties Pty Ltd v Galvin Construction Pty Ltd [2011] VSC 183** (Vickery J) — liquidated damages set-offs are "excluded amounts"; an adjudicator who takes them into account exceeds jurisdiction (old regime; LDs are now claimable/setoff-able post-reform).
7. **Maxstra Constructions Pty Ltd v Joseph Gilbert [2013] VSC 243** (Vickery J) — determinations void only "to the extent" they rest on excluded amounts; partial severance of determinations possible (old s 23(2B)).
8. **Saville v Hallmarc Constructions Pty Ltd [2015] VSCA 318** — an invoice can be a payment claim; the s 14(2)(e) endorsement ("made under this Act") and identification requirements assessed objectively and practically.
9. **SSC Plenty Road Pty Ltd v Construction Engineering (Aust) Pty Ltd [2015] VSC 631; affd [2016] VSCA 119** — authoritative exposition of the s 10A claimable-variation classes and the 10%/$5M/$150k mechanics (old regime); also addressed new reasons in adjudication responses under old s 21(2B).
10. **Façade Treatment Engineering Pty Ltd (in liq) v Brookfield Multiplex Constructions Pty Ltd [2016] VSCA 247** — a company in liquidation cannot invoke ss 15–16 (inconsistency with Corporations Act mutual set-off, s 553C); SOPA is for cash-flow of going concerns.
11. **Punton's Shoes Pty Ltd v Citi-Con (Vic) Pty Ltd [2020] VSC 514** (Digby J) — only one payment claim per reference date; a second claim for the same reference date was invalid and the determination void (old regime; now see s 14D one-claim-per-named-month default).
12. **Yuanda Vic Pty Ltd v Façade Designs International Pty Ltd [2021] VSCA 44** — where a claim included an excluded amount, the court could not sever it and enter judgment for the balance under s 16(4); claim-wide consequence (old regime; the s 16(4) excluded-amount precondition has been repealed).

Practical note for the Reviewer: with ss 10A–10B repealed and the new-reasons ban introduced, NSW/QLD authorities on claim identification and schedule adequacy (e.g. *Multiplex v Luikens*) become the closest persuasive guidance for the new Victorian regime.

## 3. Payment schedules (for Payment Schedule Reviewer)

### s 15 requirements (current)

s 15(1): a person on whom a payment claim is served (the respondent) "may reply to the claim by serving a payment schedule on the claimant." (2025 change: "providing" → "serving".)

s 15(2)–(3) — verbatim (operative parts):
> "(2) A payment schedule—
> (a) must identify the payment claim to which it relates; and
> (b) must indicate the amount of the payment (if any) that the respondent proposes to make (the scheduled amount); and …
> (d) must be in the relevant prescribed form (if any); and
> (e) must contain the prescribed information (if any).
> (3) If the scheduled amount is less than the claimed amount, the schedule must indicate why the scheduled amount is less and (if it is less because the respondent is withholding payment for any reason) the respondent's reasons for withholding payment."

- Old s 15(2)(c) (identify alleged excluded amounts) **repealed** by No. 43/2025 s. 17(2). No form/information currently prescribed.

### Time to serve

s 15(4): the respondent becomes liable to pay the claimed amount on the due date if it does not serve a payment schedule "(i) within the time required by the relevant construction contract; or (ii) within 10 business days after the payment claim is served; **whichever time expires earlier**". **Verified: 10 business days / contract period, whichever is earlier** — unchanged by the 2025 reforms (but remember the new business day definition, and s 14A(5)(c): for a prematurely served claim the clock starts on the earliest permissible service day).

### Consequences of no schedule

- s 15(4): automatic liability for the full claimed amount on the due date.
- s 16: if also unpaid by the due date, claimant may (a)(i) sue for the unpaid claimed amount as a statutory debt (no cross-claim, no contract defences — s 16(4)), or (a)(ii) go to adjudication under s 18(1)(b); plus may give notice of intention to suspend (s 16(2)(b), suspension after 3 BD: s 29).
- The old excluded-amounts precondition to judgment (old s 16(4)(a)(ii), the *Yuanda* trap) is repealed — s 16(4)(a) now only requires the court to be satisfied of the s 16(1) circumstances.
- **CRITICAL (new): reasons not in the schedule are dead for adjudication.** s 21(2)(d): the adjudication response "may not contain a reason why the respondent did not— (i) include the whole of the claimed amount in the payment schedule served on the claimant under section 15 or 18(2A) if that reason was not set out in the payment schedule". s 22(5AA) bars such reasons in further submissions; s 23(2A) forbids the adjudicator taking them into account and s 23(2B)(b) makes the determination **void to the extent** it is based on such a matter. This reverses Victoria's old position (old s 21(2B) allowed new reasons subject to a notice-and-comment procedure).
- A respondent who never serves a schedule (within s 15(4) or the s 18(2A) second chance) **cannot lodge an adjudication response at all** (s 21(2A)).

### s 18 second-chance notice regime (current)

- Where no schedule was served and the claim is unpaid, the claimant cannot apply for adjudication under s 18(1)(b) unless it first serves a notice (prescribed form, if any) on the respondent **within 10 business days after the due date for payment**, informing the respondent that (a) the claimant intends to apply for adjudication, and (b) "the respondent may serve a payment schedule on the claimant in response to the notice within 5 business days after receiving the notice" (s 18(2)).
- s 18(2A): the respondent may then serve a schedule complying with s 15(2)–(3) **within 5 business days** after receiving the notice. (Old law gave only **2 business days** — the window was extended in 2026.)
- The adjudication application must then be lodged **within 5 business days after the end of that 5-business-day period** (s 18(3)(e)).
- Note: the second-chance notice route applies only to the **no-schedule** pathway. (The s 16 court-debt route does not require the s 18(2) notice.)

### Adequacy-of-reasons case law

Victorian authority on schedule adequacy is comparatively thin because old s 21(2B) allowed respondents to raise new reasons at adjudication; from 15 April 2026 the schedule is the respondent's one shot, so adequacy doctrine (largely NSW-derived) is now central.

1. **Multiplex Constructions Pty Ltd v Luikens [2003] NSWSC 1140** (Palmer J) — leading persuasive authority: reasons may be brief and abbreviated but must be sufficient to apprise the claimant of the real issues in dispute so it can decide whether to adjudicate; parties' shared background knowledge is taken into account.
2. **Clarence Street Pty Ltd v Isis Projects Pty Ltd [2005] NSWCA 391** — claims and schedules are to be read practically, not with overly technical scrutiny; supports a merits-based adequacy assessment.
3. **Pacific General Securities Ltd v Soliman & Sons Pty Ltd [2006] NSWSC 13** — even where the respondent's schedule/response is deficient or absent, the adjudicator must still be satisfied of the claimant's entitlement on the material; no automatic win.
4. **John Goss Projects Pty Ltd v Leighton Contractors Pty Ltd [2006] NSWSC 798** — meaning of "reasons included in the payment schedule"; new reasons outside the schedule cannot be relied on (the position Victoria has now legislated).
5. **470 St Kilda Road Pty Ltd v Reed Constructions Australia Pty Ltd [2012] VSC 235** (Vickery J) — Victorian treatment of the schedule/adjudication interface and enforcement of determinations; respondent held to the case it advanced in the payment process.
6. **Saville v Hallmarc Constructions Pty Ltd [2015] VSCA 318** — a responsive document not labelled "payment schedule" can qualify if, objectively, it identifies the claim and conveys the amount (including nil) the respondent proposes to pay and why.
7. **Amasya Enterprises Pty Ltd v Asta Developments (Aust) Pty Ltd [2015] VSC 233; and [2016] VSC 326** (Vickery J) — judicial review for jurisdictional error survives entry of judgment; under old s 21(2B)–(2C) the adjudicator had to give the claimant notice and an opportunity to respond to new reasons — procedural-fairness holdings that inform the new, stricter regime.
8. **SSC Plenty Road v Construction Engineering (Aust) Pty Ltd [2016] VSCA 119** — confirms adjudicator's treatment of reasons and the limits of review; useful on what counts as a reason "duly made" in support of a schedule.

## 4. Due dates (for Due Date Calculator)

All periods are **business days** (new definition: excludes Sat/Sun, statewide public holidays, and **22 December–10 January inclusive**). "Named month" = calendar month.

| Event | Section | Rule (current, claims served on/after 15 Apr 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Earliest day to serve payment claim | s 14A(1) | Last day of the named month in which work first done, and last day of each subsequent month in which work done. December: work 1–21 Dec → from 22 Dec; work 22–31 Dec → from 31 Jan. Contract may allow earlier (s 14A(3)); termination day if contract terminated (s 14A(4)). Early service deemed served on earliest day (s 14A(5)). |
| Latest day to serve payment claim | s 14C | No later than **the day before** the latest of: contract latest date; **6 months after practical completion** of all work; 6 months after supply of all related goods/services. |
| Claim frequency | s 14D | One claim per named month (default); unpaid amounts may be re-claimed. |
| **Due date for payment** | s 12(1) | (a) the day payment becomes due and payable **under the contract** (subject to (1B)); or (b) if the contract does not expressly provide, **10 business days after the earliest day on which a payment claim may be served under s 14A** for that payment (NB: anchored to the earliest permissible service day, not actual service). |
| Statutory cap on payment terms | s 12(1B) | A contract term has **no effect** to the extent it provides for payment "later than the day that is **20 business days** after— (a) a payment claim is served". So: contract due date applies, capped at 20 BD after actual service of the claim. |
| **Payment schedule deadline** | s 15(4) | Earlier of: time required by the contract, or **10 business days after the payment claim is served**. For prematurely served claims, the clock starts on the earliest permissible day (s 14A(5)(c)). |
| Second-chance notice (no schedule, unpaid) | s 18(2) | Claimant must serve notice **within 10 business days after the due date**; respondent may serve a schedule **within 5 business days** after receiving the notice (s 18(2A)). |
| **Adjudication application — scheduled amount < claimed** (s 18(1)(a)(i)) | s 18(3)(c) | Lodge with ANA **within 10 business days after the claimant receives the payment schedule**. |
| **Adjudication application — scheduled amount unpaid** (s 18(1)(a)(ii)) | s 18(3)(d) | Lodge **within 10 business days after the due date for payment**. (Old law: 20 BD — now 10.) |
| **Adjudication application — no schedule & unpaid** (s 18(1)(b)) | s 18(3)(e) | Lodge **within 5 business days after the end of the 5-business-day period** in s 18(2)(b). (Sequence: due date → ≤10 BD notice → 5 BD respondent window → 5 BD to lodge.) |
| Serve copy of application on respondent | s 18(5) | **Within 3 business days** after lodging with the ANA. |
| ANA refers to adjudicator | s 18(7) | "as soon as practicable" (no fixed days). |
| Adjudicator acceptance backstop | s 28(1)(a) | If no notice of acceptance within **4 business days** after the adjudicator receives the application, claimant may withdraw and re-apply **within 5 business days** of becoming so entitled (s 28(2)–(3)). |
| **Adjudication response** | s 21(1) | Later of: **5 business days** after receiving the copy of the application, or **2 business days** after receiving notice of the adjudicator's acceptance. Only available if a schedule was served in time (s 21(2A)); no new reasons (s 21(2)(d)). Copy to claimant within **3 business days** of lodging (s 21(3)). |
| **Adjudicator's determination** | s 22(4) | **Within 10 business days** after the **latest** of: (i) the day the adjudicator is taken to be appointed (s 20 acceptance), or (ii) the latest day the respondent may lodge a response; **or** within further time **not exceeding 20 business days** after that period, **agreed by both claimant and respondent** (i.e. max ~30 BD). Agreement not to be unreasonably withheld (s 22(4A)). A late determination is **not invalid solely for lateness** (s 22(4B), new). If not determined in time, claimant may withdraw and re-apply within 5 BD (s 28). |
| ANA gives determination to parties | s 23A | "as soon as practicable" (to the Authority within 5 business days). |
| **Payment/release of adjudicated amount** | s 28M | On or before the **"relevant date"**: **5 business days after the date a copy of the determination is given to the respondent** under s 23A, or any later date determined by the adjudicator under s 23(1)(b). |
| Suspension of work | s 29(1) | Permitted once **at least 3 business days** have passed since notice of intention (under s 16, 17 or 28O). Must resume within 3 BD after payment (or contract period ≥1 BD) (s 29(2)). |
| **Review adjudication** | — | **Abolished.** Division 2A (ss 28A–28L) repealed by No. 43/2025 s. 28 on 15 April 2026. (Legacy only, for review applications made before commencement: threshold adjudicated amount > $100,000; ground = excluded amounts; application within 5 BD of receiving the determination; review determination within 5 BD of appointment, extendable ≤10 BD; payment 5 BD.) There is **no review mechanism in the current Act** — challenge is by judicial review for jurisdictional error. |
| Service by post | s 50(2)(c) | Taken served **7 business days** after posting (or actual earlier delivery). Email: on receipt (Electronic Transactions (Victoria) Act 2000 s 13A, per Note to s 50(2)). Service on ANAs may be by e-lockbox upload (reg 11, from 3 June 2026). |

Performance security stream (new Division 1A — relevant if the calculators are extended): earliest release claim — the earliest of **20 business days after the end of the relevant defects liability period** or a contract-specified day/event (s 17B(1)); latest — the later of the contract date or the last day of the named month following the month the last DLP ends (s 17C); performance security schedule — earlier of contract time or **10 business days** after the claim (s 17E(4)); due date for release — contract date or, if silent, **10 business days after the earliest day the claim could be served**, capped at **20 business days after the claim is served** (s 12(1A)–(1B)); adjudication under s 18A mirrors s 18 (10/10/5+5 BD; copy 3 BD); **recourse to security requires ≥5 business days' written notice** identifying contract, provisions relied on, amount and circumstances (s 17H, cannot be contracted out, applies to existing securities).

**Legacy due dates (claims served before 15 Apr 2026)**: due date = contract date or 10 BD after the claim is *made* (old s 12(1), no cap); schedule = earlier of contract/10 BD (same); applications: 10 BD after schedule / **20 BD** after due date / 5 BD after the **2-business-day** second-chance window; response 5 BD/2 BD (same); determination 10 BD after acceptance, extendable to 15 BD with the claimant's agreement only; old business day definition (no Christmas shutdown — only Sat/Sun/public holidays).

## 5. Interest (for Interest Calculator)

- **s 12(2)** (unchanged in substance) — verbatim:
  > "(2) Interest is payable on the unpaid amount of a progress payment that has become due and payable in accordance with subsection (1) at the greater of the following rates—
  > (a) the rate for the time being fixed under section 2 of the Penalty Interest Rates Act 1983; or
  > (b) the rate specified under the construction contract."
- New **s 12(3)**: interest "may be payable" on the unpaid amount of a performance security due to be released, at the greater of the same two rates.
- The adjudicator must determine the interest rate payable per s 23(1)(c) (and s 23(1A)(d) for securities); unpaid interest can be added to the adjudication certificate and becomes part of the adjudicated amount (s 28Q(2)–(3)). Judgment-debt interest statutes are unaffected (s 28R(5)).
- **Penalty interest rate (PIRA s 2)**: fixed by the Attorney-General by notice in the Government Gazette. Current rate: **10% per annum, in force since 1 February 2017** — still current as at 10 June 2026 per the Supreme Court of Victoria penalty interest rates page and DJCS penalties page (no later gazette found). Immediately preceding rates: 9.5% (1 Jun 2015–31 Jan 2017), 10.5% (11 Aug 2014), 11.5% (3 Feb 2014), 10% (7 Oct 2013).
- Calculator logic: simple interest at max(10% p.a. [or gazetted rate at the time], contract rate) on the unpaid amount, from the day after the s 12(1) due date until payment. Build the rate as a dated lookup table in case a new gazette notice changes it.

## 6. Differences vs QLD BIF Act (practically important)

- **Excluded amounts**: Historically the defining Victorian difference — variations (unless "claimable"), time-related costs, latent conditions, regulatory-change costs and damages were unclaimable (ss 10A–10B). **Repealed 15 April 2026** — Victoria has converged with QLD: delay costs, disputed variations and damages-type amounts can now be claimed/scheduled. Legacy claims (served pre-15 Apr 2026) still carry the old rules, including the *Yuanda* bar on court judgment where a claim includes an excluded amount.
- **Reference dates**: QLD (BIF s 67) still uses reference dates (default last day of the month). Victoria has **abolished** reference dates entirely — a statutory monthly entitlement (claims from the last day of each named month) with the December carve-outs.
- **Endorsement**: Vic claims **must state they are made under the Act** (s 14(2)(e)). QLD removed the endorsement requirement under BIF (a document claiming payment can qualify without it). For Vic templates, the endorsement is mandatory.
- **Latest claim**: Vic = day before 6 months after practical completion (or longer contract period). QLD = generally the longer of the contract period or 6 months after the work was last carried out (final claims: longer of contract period / 28 days after end of DLP / 6 months after completion). Different anchors — don't reuse the QLD logic.
- **Payment schedule deadline**: Vic = earlier of contract or **10 BD**; QLD = earlier of contract or **15 BD**.
- **No schedule consequence**: QLD — failure to give a schedule is an offence (penalty units) and the claimant may sue or adjudicate directly (no second chance). Vic — no offence; instead the **second-chance notice** regime (s 18(2): claimant notice within 10 BD of due date; respondent gets 5 BD to schedule) is a mandatory precondition to adjudicating a no-schedule claim. Sopal's QLD flow has no equivalent step.
- **New reasons at adjudication**: now aligned — both ban reasons not in the payment schedule (Vic ss 21(2)(d), 22(5AA), 23(2A); QLD BIF s 82(4)). Before 15 Apr 2026 Vic uniquely allowed new reasons with a notice procedure.
- **Due date / payment terms cap**: Vic — contract date or 10 BD after the earliest permissible claim-service day if contract silent; statutory **cap of 20 BD after service** (s 12(1B)) applies to all construction contracts. QLD — 10 BD default under BIF; caps (15 BD head contracts / 25 BD subcontracts) come from QBCC Act ss 67U/67W and only for "building contracts". Different cap architecture and numbers.
- **Adjudication application windows**: Vic 10/10/5(+5) BD (see §4). QLD 30 BD (schedule disputes / no-schedule route) and 20 BD (unpaid scheduled amount) — QLD windows are far longer; do not transplant.
- **Adjudicator appointment**: Vic — claimant chooses a private **ANA** which nominates the adjudicator. QLD — application to the **QBCC Adjudication Registrar**. Affects lodgement instructions in the tools.
- **Decision deadline**: Vic — 10 BD from appointment/response window close, extendable up to +20 BD by **both parties'** agreement; late decision not invalid (s 22(4B)). QLD — 10 BD (standard) / 15 BD (complex) from response, extendable by agreement; a late QLD decision is void (BIF s 85(5)) — opposite consequence.
- **Response window**: Vic — later of 5 BD after application copy / 2 BD after acceptance notice. QLD — 10 BD (standard) / 15 BD (complex, extendable). Vic is much tighter.
- **Business days**: both jurisdictions now exclude the 22 December–10 January shutdown (QLD BIF already did; Vic adopted it from 15 Apr 2026) — but Vic also excludes days "wholly or partly" observed as public holidays **throughout Victoria**, and Vic's December claim-service deferral rules (s 14A(2)) have no QLD equivalent.
- **Performance security**: Vic now has a full statutory stream — release claims (ss 17A–17G), adjudication of release (s 18A), 20 BD release cap (s 12(1B)), and the **5 BD notice before recourse** (s 17H). QLD's BIF Act has no recourse-notice/release-claim regime (QLD instead has statutory trusts for retention under its trust-account framework). Entirely new module territory for Sopal.
- **Notice-based time bars**: Vic s 13A lets adjudicators, courts, arbitrators or contract experts declare unfair time bars of no effect (onus on the party alleging unfairness). No QLD BIF equivalent (WA's 2021 Act is the model).
- **Review adjudication**: neither jurisdiction now has one (Vic's excluded-amounts review, Div 2A, was repealed 15 Apr 2026).
- **Interest**: Vic — greater of penalty interest rate (10% since 1 Feb 2017) and contract rate (s 12(2)). QLD — greater of the Civil Proceedings Act 2011 (Qld) court rate and the contract rate. Different reference rates; Vic's is fixed by gazette, QLD's floats with the court practice rate.
- **Domestic building carve-out**: Vic excludes owner-occupier domestic building contracts (s 7(2)(b)–(ba)); QLD BIF covers a broader span (with its own owner-occupier carve-outs). Keep separate eligibility gates.
- **Claim frequency**: Vic — one claim per named month default (s 14D), with express right to re-claim unpaid amounts. QLD — one claim per reference date, similar re-claim right (BIF s 75(4)).
- **Retrospectivity**: the Vic reforms apply to contracts whenever entered (s 54(1)) — for contract-vs-Act conflict checks, the contract date does NOT matter for Vic (unlike most QLD BIF transitional analysis); what matters is whether the claim was served before or on/after 15 April 2026.
