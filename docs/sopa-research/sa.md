# South Australia — Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 2009

Research current as at **10 June 2026**. Primary text taken from the authorised consolidated PDF published on legislation.sa.gov.au (Act version **10.12.2011** — the Act has **never been amended** since commencement; the only change to the regime is the **1 May 2025** amendment to the Regulations). All statutory quotations below are verbatim from the authorised versions (PDF text-extraction line-break artifacts removed; wording untouched).

## 1. Act metadata

- **Full title:** *Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 2009* (SA) — Act No 77 of 2009. Assent 10.12.2009; commenced **10 December 2011**.
- **Suggested citation form for Sopal:** "s 13 SOP Act (SA)" or "s 13 of the *Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 2009* (SA)". Note the SA short title has no parentheses around "Security of Payment" (unlike the ACT Act).
- **Current consolidation:** Version 10.12.2011 is still the current authorised version (legislation.sa.gov.au lists only two versions: current, and historical 10.12.2009–09.12.2011 pre-commencement). The Act's legislative history table shows the principal Act only — **no amending Act has ever passed**. Reform Bills in 2017 and 2021 both lapsed (see §6 note).
- **Regulations:** *Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Regulations 2011* (SA) (No 251 of 2011), as amended by the *Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment (Exemption) Amendment Regulations 2025* (SA) (No 17 of 2025, Gazette 1.5.2025 p 834, commenced **1 May 2025**). Current regs version dates from 1 May 2025.
- **Transitional:** Sch 1 Pt 4 cl 4: "This Act does not apply to a construction contract entered into before the commencement of this Act." (i.e. contracts entered into before 10 December 2011).
- **Administration:** The Small Business Commission SA (formerly Small Business Commissioner) administers/supports the Act in practice; adjudicators are nominated by **authorised nominating authorities (ANAs)** authorised by the Minister under s 29 (the NSW-style ANA model — QLD's registrar-appointment model does NOT apply). ANAs listed by SBC SA (page sighted June 2026): ABC Dispute Resolution Services, Adjudicate Today, Australian Solutions Centre, Resolution Institute, RICS Dispute Resolution Service.
- **Model:** SA is a close copy of the **pre-2019 NSW Act** (East Coast model), with SA-specific day counts (15/15 instead of NSW's 10/10) and an SA-specific business-day definition.

### Key definitions (s 4 — Interpretation)

**business day** (s 4):
> "*business day* means any day other than—
> (a) a Saturday, Sunday or public holiday; or
> (b) 27, 28, 29, 30 or 31 December; or
> (c) any other day on which there is a Statewide shut-down of the operations of the building and construction industry;"

Notes for the Due Date Calculator:
- Public holidays = **South Australian** public holidays (including SA-specific ones, e.g. Adelaide Cup Day (2nd Monday in March), and SA's part-day public holidays regime is irrelevant for day-counting since those days are public holidays only in part — flag: SA proclaims 24 and 31 December *part-day* public holidays from 7pm; 31 December is already excluded by para (b); 24 December is **not** an excluded day).
- Excluded December days: **27–31 December only** (cf NSW 27–31 Dec also; QLD excludes 22 Dec–10 Jan). 25 and 26 December are public holidays anyway. 1 January caught as public holiday.
- Para (c) (Statewide industry shut-down) has no published proclamation mechanism — treat as a manual flag, not calculable.

**reference date** (s 4 — NB: defined in s 4 in SA, **not** s 8(2) as in NSW):
> "*reference date*, in relation to a construction contract, means—
> (a) a date determined by or in accordance with the terms of the contract as the date on which a claim for a progress payment may be made in relation to work carried out or undertaken to be carried out (or related goods and services supplied or undertaken to be supplied) under the contract; or
> (b) if the contract makes no express provision with respect to the matter—the last day of the named month in which the construction work was first carried out (or the related goods and services were first supplied) under the contract and the last day of each subsequent named month;"

**progress payment** (s 4): includes, without affecting the s 8 entitlement —
> "(a) the final payment for construction work carried out (or for related goods and services supplied) under a construction contract; and
> (b) a single or one-off payment for carrying out construction work (or for supplying related goods and services) under a construction contract; and
> (c) a payment that is based on an event or date (known in the building and construction industry as a "milestone payment");"

Other s 4 definitions track NSW: *claimant*, *respondent*, *claimed amount*, *scheduled amount*, *adjudicated amount*, *construction contract* ("a contract or other arrangement under which 1 party undertakes to carry out construction work, or to supply related goods and services, for another party"), *recognised financial institution* (extended by reg 4 to all APRA-regulated bodies).

- **construction work**: s 5 (standard list; excludes drilling for oil/gas and extraction of minerals — s 5(2)).
- **related goods and services**: s 6; **reg 5(1)** prescribes additionally "(a) project management services in relation to construction work; (b) contract management services in relation to construction work; (c) consultancy services in relation to construction work" — but **reg 5(2)** (inserted 1 May 2025) provides "Subregulation (1) does not apply to any services provided under a prescribed contract (within the meaning of regulation 7)."

## 2. Payment claims (for Payment Claim Reviewer)

### s 13 validity requirements

s 13(1): a person referred to in s 8 "who is or who claims to be entitled to a progress payment (the *claimant*) may serve a payment claim on the person who, under the contract concerned, is or may be liable to make the payment."

s 13(2) (verbatim):
> "(2) A payment claim—
> (a) must identify the construction work (or related goods and services) to which the progress payment relates; and
> (b) must indicate the amount of the progress payment that the claimant claims to be due (the *claimed amount*); and
> (c) must state that it is made under this Act."

**The endorsement requirement (s 13(2)(c)) is mandatory in SA** — unlike QLD (BIF removed it) and unlike NSW 2014–2019 (NSW removed then partly restored it). A claim without the statement "This is a payment claim made under the Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 2009 (SA)" (or words to that effect) is not a valid payment claim.

s 13(3): the claimed amount may include an amount "(a) that the respondent is liable to pay the claimant under section 28(3); or (b) that is held under the construction contract by the respondent and that the claimant claims is due for release" — i.e. **retention/security release amounts are expressly claimable**.

### Reference dates

- Entitlement: s 8 — "On and from each reference date under a construction contract, a person— (a) who has undertaken to carry out construction work under the contract; or (b) who has undertaken to supply related goods and services under the contract, is entitled to a progress payment."
- Reference date definition is in **s 4** (SA quirk — the NSW equivalent is s 8(2); SA moved it to the interpretation section). Default: **last day of the named month** of first work and the last day of each subsequent named month.
- A valid reference date is an essential precondition to a valid payment claim: *Southern Han Breakfast Point Pty Ltd (in liq) v Lewence Construction Pty Ltd* [2016] HCA 52 (on the identically structured NSW Act) — highly persuasive in SA.

### Service window — s 13(4) (verbatim)

> "(4) A payment claim may be served only within—
> (a) the period determined by or in accordance with the terms of the construction contract; or
> (b) the period of 6 months after the construction work to which the claim relates was last carried out (or the related goods and services to which the claim relates were last supplied),
> whichever is the later."

So: **later of the contract period and 6 months after work last carried out** (same structure as pre-2019 NSW s 13(4); QLD BIF s 75(2)–(3) differs with its longer-of rule plus special 28-day final-claim rule, which SA does not have). Note SBC SA's FAQ paraphrases this as a bare 6-month rule — the Act's "whichever is the later" governs.

### One claim per reference date

s 13(5)–(6) (verbatim):
> "(5) A claimant cannot serve more than 1 payment claim in respect of each reference date under the construction contract.
> (6) However, subsection (5) does not prevent the claimant from including in a payment claim an amount that has been the subject of a previous claim."

- Re-agitation of claims **determined by an adjudicator** is not permitted (issue estoppel / abuse of process): *Dualcorp Pty Ltd v Remo Constructions Pty Ltd* [2009] NSWCA 69, applied in SA in *Goyder Wind Farm 1 Pty Ltd v GE Renewable Energy Australia Pty Ltd* [2024] SASC 108 (injunction granted against adjudicating a claim that re-agitated "thickening costs" already determined; but different cost components of the same delay event may be claimed across successive claims if there is no overlap).
- s 22(4): a subsequent adjudicator must give work the same value as previously determined unless satisfied the value has changed — *Goyder* holds s 22(4) is **not jurisdictional**.

### Exclusions (s 7 — current position, verified June 2026)

- s 7(2)(b) — **residential owner-occupier carve-out**: the Act does not apply to "a construction contract for the carrying out of domestic building work (within the meaning of the *Building Work Contractors Act 1995*) on such part of any premises that the party for whom the work is carried out resides in or proposes to reside in". (Builder-to-subcontractor contracts on residential projects ARE covered; only the contract with the resident/intending-resident owner is excluded.)
- s 7(2)(a): contracts forming part of loan/guarantee/insurance arrangements with a recognised financial institution.
- s 7(2)(c): contracts where consideration is **not calculated by reference to the value of the work** (e.g. true cost-plus? — note this mirrors old NSW s 7(2)(c); construe narrowly).
- s 7(3)(a): employment contracts (Fair Work Act 1994 (SA) employees).
- s 7(4): work carried out outside SA.
- **Crown exemption (in force 1 May 2025)** — new **reg 7** (made under s 35(2)), verbatim:
  > "(1) Pursuant to section 35(2) of the Act, a party to a prescribed contract is exempt from the operation of the following provisions of the Act in respect of that contract:
  > (a) section 14(4); (b) section 15; (c) section 16(2)(a)(ii) and (4); (d) Part 3 Divisions 2 and 3; (e) section 30; (f) section 31.
  > (2) In this regulation—
  > *Crown* includes a Minister, agency or instrumentality of the Crown;
  > *prescribed contract* means a contract (whether entered into before or after the commencement of this regulation) entered into between the Crown and another person under which the total amount payable to the other person will exceed $4 000 000 (excluding GST) over the term of the contract;
  > *term of the contract* does not include any extension of the contract."

  Practical effect for the reviewers/calculators: on Crown head contracts > $4m (ex GST), the deemed-liability rule (s 14(4)), the no-schedule debt-recovery provision (s 15), adjudication (Pt 3 Div 2), and suspension (Pt 3 Div 3) are **switched off**; the entitlement provisions (ss 8–13, payment schedules s 14(1)–(3), due date s 11 and interest) remain. **Retrospective** to pre-1 May 2025 contracts. Status flag: the SA Legislative Review Committee took evidence on these regulations on 12 Nov 2025 (witnesses incl John Murray AM, the Small Business Commissioner, DIT); as at the sources checked (June 2026) no disallowance was identified — worth re-verifying before shipping SA support.
- Sub-$4m Crown contracts: the Act binds the Crown in practice (no general Crown carve-out in the Act itself).

Leading SA authority on judicial review of determinations. |

## 3. Payment schedules (for Payment Schedule Reviewer)

### s 14 content requirements (verbatim)

> "(1) A person on whom a payment claim is served (the *respondent*) may reply to the claim by providing a payment schedule to the claimant.
> (2) A payment schedule—
> (a) must identify the payment claim to which it relates; and
> (b) must indicate the amount of the payment (if any) that the respondent proposes to make (the *scheduled amount*).
> (3) If the scheduled amount is less than the claimed amount, the schedule must indicate why the scheduled amount is less and (if it is less because the respondent is withholding payment for any reason) the respondent's reasons for withholding payment."

### Time to provide — s 14(4) (verbatim)

> "(4) If—
> (a) a claimant serves a payment claim on a respondent; and
> (b) the respondent does not provide a payment schedule to the claimant—
> (i) within the time required by the relevant construction contract; or
> (ii) within 15 business days after the payment claim is served,
> whichever time expires earlier,
> the respondent becomes liable to pay the claimed amount to the claimant on the due date for the progress payment to which the payment claim relates."

**Confirmed: 15 business days or the contract period, whichever expires earlier** (NSW = 10 bd; QLD BIF s 76 = earlier of contract period and 15 bd — same count as SA).

### Consequences of no schedule / non-payment

- **No schedule within time → deemed liability** for the full claimed amount on the due date (s 14(4)).
- s 15 (no schedule + no payment): claimant may (a)(i) recover the unpaid claimed amount as a **debt due** in court, or (a)(ii) make an adjudication application under s 17(1)(b); and (b) serve notice of intention to suspend. In debt proceedings the respondent "is not... entitled— (i) to bring a cross-claim against the claimant; or (ii) to raise a defence in relation to matters arising under the construction contract" (s 15(4)(b)).
- s 16 (schedule given but scheduled amount unpaid): debt recovery or adjudication under s 17(1)(a)(ii), plus suspension notice; same cross-claim/defence bar (s 16(4)).
- **Crown contracts > $4m**: s 14(4), s 15 and s 16(2)(a)(ii)/(4) are disapplied by reg 7 (from 1 May 2025) — no deemed liability and no adjudication; the claimant is left to ordinary contract remedies.
- No offence for failing to schedule (cf QLD BIF s 76(2) — offence; and QLD's s 99 obligation to pay). SA has no equivalent.

### s 17(2) second-chance notice regime (verbatim)

> "(2) An adjudication application to which subsection (1)(b) applies cannot be made unless—
> (a) the claimant has notified the respondent, within the period of 20 business days immediately following the due date for payment, of the claimant's intention to apply for adjudication of the payment claim; and
> (b) the respondent has been given an opportunity to provide a payment schedule to the claimant within 5 business days after receiving the claimant's notice."

I.e. where **no payment schedule** was provided and the claim is unpaid, the claimant must give a "second chance" notice within **20 business days after the due date**, and the respondent then has **5 business days** to provide a (late) payment schedule. A schedule provided under s 17(2)(b) preserves the respondent's right to lodge an adjudication response (s 20(3)). This mirrors pre-amendment NSW s 17(2); **QLD abolished the second-chance regime** in the BIF Act.

### Adequacy-of-reasons case law

- *Multiplex Constructions Pty Ltd v Luikens* [2003] NSWSC 1140 at [76]–[78]: reasons may be terse; the schedule must convey enough to apprise the claimant of the true ground of dispute, because the respondent is later confined to those reasons (the leading adequacy authority; persuasive in SA).
- s 20(4) SA (verbatim): "The respondent cannot include in the adjudication response reasons for withholding payment unless those reasons have already been included in the payment schedule provided to the claimant." — the SA mirror of NSW s 20(2B); *John Holland Pty Ltd v Roads and Traffic Authority of NSW* [2007] NSWCA 19 on the operation of the new-reasons bar.
- *Clarence Street Pty Ltd v Isis Projects Pty Ltd* [2005] NSWCA 391 — identification/responsiveness standard.
- *Perform (NSW) Pty Ltd v MEV-AUS Pty Ltd* [2009] NSWCA 157 — adjudicator may consider submissions "duly made"; relationship between schedule reasons and adjudication.
- *Brookhollow Pty Ltd v R & R Consultants Pty Ltd* [2006] NSWSC 1 — consequences where no/invalid schedule; adjudicator's task on a s 17(1)(b) application.
- SA-specific overlay: *Built Environs v Tali* [2013] SASC 84 — natural justice limits on an adjudicator deciding on grounds not advanced in the schedule/submissions.

## 4. Due dates (for Due Date Calculator)

All counts are in SA **business days** (see definition §1; excludes Sat/Sun, SA public holidays, 27–31 December, and any Statewide industry shut-down day).

| Event | Section | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Due date for progress payment | **s 11(1)** | Date payable per the contract; if the contract makes no express provision — "the date occurring **15 business days** after a payment claim is made under Part 3 in relation to the payment". (Verbatim: "(a) on the date on which the payment becomes due and payable in accordance with the terms of the contract; or (b) if the contract makes no express provision with respect to the matter—on the date occurring 15 business days after a payment claim is made under Part 3 in relation to the payment.") **No statutory cap** on longer contractual terms (cf QLD QBCC Act s 67U/67W caps; NSW 2019 caps). |
| Payment schedule | **s 14(4)** | Earlier of: time required by the contract, and **15 business days** after the payment claim is served. |
| Adjudication application — scheduled amount < claimed amount | **s 17(3)(c)** | "must be made within **15 business days** after the claimant receives the payment schedule" (s 17(1)(a)(i) scenario). |
| Adjudication application — scheduled amount not paid in full by due date | **s 17(3)(d)** | "must be made within **20 business days** after the due date for payment" (s 17(1)(a)(ii) scenario). |
| Adjudication application — no schedule and no payment | **s 17(2) + s 17(3)(e)** | Two-stage: (1) notice of intention within **20 business days** immediately following the due date for payment; (2) respondent gets **5 business days** after receiving the notice to provide a schedule; (3) application "must be made within **15 business days** after the end of the 5 day period referred to in subsection (2)(b)" (s 17(3)(e) — note the Act says "5 day period", referring to the 5-business-day period in s 17(2)(b)). |
| Copy of application to respondent | s 17(5) | "A copy of an adjudication application must be served on the respondent concerned." (No fixed day count.) |
| Adjudication response | **s 20(1)** | Later of: **5 business days** after receiving the copy of the application, and **2 business days** after receiving notice of the adjudicator's acceptance. Respondent may respond only if it provided a schedule within s 14(4) or s 17(2)(b) time (s 20(3)); no new reasons (s 20(4)). |
| Adjudicator's determination | **s 21(3)** | "within 10 business days after— (i) the date on which an adjudication response is lodged with the adjudicator; or (ii) if an adjudication response is not lodged... the last date on which the response may be lodged...; or (iii) if the respondent is not entitled under section 20 to lodge an adjudication response—the date on which the respondent receives a copy of the adjudication application; or (b) within any further time that the claimant and the respondent may agree." **10 business days** from the response (or last day for it, or service of the application where no response right). NB: SA runs from the response date, not from acceptance (NSW runs 10 bd from notification of acceptance). |
| Payment of adjudicated amount | s 23 | On or before the "relevant date": **5 business days** after the determination is served, or any later date determined under s 22(1)(b). |
| New application where adjudicator fails | s 26 | If no notice of acceptance within **4 business days** of application, or no determination in time, or adjudicator withdraws: new application within **5 business days** of entitlement arising (s 26(4)), overriding s 17(3)(c)–(e). |
| Suspension of work | s 28(1) | At least **2 business days** after notice of intention given under s 15, 16 or 24; right continues until **3 business days** after payment received (s 28(2)). |

**Business day exclusions verified:** SA excludes **27, 28, 29, 30 and 31 December** (s 4) — not the QLD 22 Dec–10 Jan window. Plus Saturdays, Sundays, public holidays (SA), and "any other day on which there is a Statewide shut-down of the operations of the building and construction industry".

## 5. Interest (for Interest Calculator)

**s 11(2) (verbatim):**
> "(2) Interest is payable on the unpaid amount of a progress payment that has become due and payable at the rate—
> (a) prescribed under the *Supreme Court Act 1935* in respect of judgment debts of the Supreme Court; or
> (b) specified under the construction contract,
> whichever is the greater."

**Important correction to the brief:** SA s 11(2) points to the **Supreme Court Act 1935 (SA)** judgment-debt rate — *not* the Enforcement of Judgments Act 1991 (that Act governs enforcement machinery; the rate is not prescribed under it).

**Mechanism chain (verified June 2026):**
1. s 11(2)(a) → rate "prescribed under the Supreme Court Act 1935 in respect of judgment debts of the Supreme Court".
2. The rate is prescribed by rules of court made under that Act — now the **Uniform Civil Rules 2020 (SA), r 185.1 (Post-judgment interest)**, verbatim:
   > "(1) Subject to subrule (2), interest on a judgment accrues on and after the commencement date at the rate of 6 per cent per annum.
   > (2) The Chief Judicial Officer may from time to time prescribe another rate of interest in respect of a period from not earlier than the first anniversary of the commencement date onwards.
   > (3) The Registrar must publish all adjustments made under subrule (2) on the CAA website."
3. **Current rate: 6% per annum, simple interest** (confirmed by the Legal Services Commission's Law Handbook SA, and CCJANZ which lists UCR r 185.1 as the SA post-judgment provision). No Chief Judicial Officer adjustment under r 185.1(2) was found published as at June 2026 — the calculator should treat 6% p.a. as the statutory floor rate but this should be re-checked against the CAA website (courts.sa.gov.au) before launch and periodically, since the rate is adjustable by publication rather than fixed in the Act.
4. The payable rate is the **greater of** 6% p.a. and the contract rate (s 11(2)(b)).

Calculator behaviour:
- Interest runs on the **unpaid amount** from (and the natural reading is from the day after) the due date under s 11(1) until payment. Simple interest, daily accrual on an annual rate, calendar days (the business-day definition affects due-date calculation, not interest accrual).
- An adjudicator must determine "the rate of interest payable" on the adjudicated amount: s 22(1)(c). Unpaid interest can be added to the adjudication certificate and becomes part of the adjudicated amount: s 24(4).
- Once an adjudication certificate is filed as a judgment (s 25), post-judgment interest accrues under UCR r 185.1 in the ordinary way (also 6% p.a. currently).
- Unlike QLD there is **no penalty-rate regime** (no QBCC Act s 67P analogue) and no offence for late payment.

## 6. Differences vs QLD BIF Act

Practically important differences (SA Act vs *Building Industry Fairness (Security of Payment) Act 2017* (Qld)):

- **Endorsement:** SA payment claims **must state they are made under the Act** (s 13(2)(c)). QLD abolished the endorsement requirement (a BIF claim just needs to identify work, state the amount and request payment; an invoice can qualify).
- **Default due date:** SA — contract date or, if silent, **15 business days** after the claim (s 11(1)); **no cap** on longer contractual payment terms. QLD — if silent, 10 business days (BIF s 73), and QBCC Act ss 67U/67W void terms longer than 15 bd (commercial building head contracts) / 25 bd (subcontracts).
- **Payment schedule window:** both are "earlier of contract period and 15 business days" (SA s 14(4); QLD BIF s 76) — same count, but QLD makes failure to give a schedule an **offence** (BIF s 76(2)) and failure to pay an offence (s 99); SA has no offences.
- **Second-chance notice:** SA retains it — no-schedule adjudications need a s 17(2) notice (20 bd window) + 5 bd late-schedule opportunity. QLD abolished the second chance: the claimant can go straight to adjudication (30 bd), and the respondent who gave no schedule cannot give an adjudication response at all.
- **Adjudication application windows:** SA — 15 bd (schedule less than claim), 20 bd (scheduled amount unpaid), 15 bd after the 5-day period (no schedule, after s 17(2) notice). QLD — 30 bd (s 79(2)(b) less-than; and s 79(2)(a) no-schedule/no-payment), 20 bd (s 79(2)(c) unpaid scheduled amount).
- **Appointment model:** SA uses claimant-chosen private **ANAs** (s 17(3)(b), s 29). QLD uses the **Adjudication Registrar at the QBCC**. ANA-selection bias is a live SA issue (*Built Environs*).
- **Decision deadline:** SA — 10 business days from response/last response day (s 21(3)), extendable only by agreement of both parties. QLD — 10 bd standard / 15 bd complex claims from response, with registrar-notified extensions and the complex-claim category. SA has **no standard/complex distinction**.
- **Business days:** SA excludes 27–31 December; QLD excludes **22 December to 10 January**. Calculators must use SA public holidays.
- **Reference dates:** SA retains reference dates (s 4 definition; one claim per reference date, s 13(5)). QLD also retains them (BIF s 67), but QLD has a statutory final-claim window (longer of contract period / 28 days after end of defects liability period / 6 months) — SA has only the s 13(4) later-of rule (contract period / 6 months).
- **Interest:** SA — greater of contract rate and the Supreme Court judgment-debt rate (currently **6% p.a.** via UCR r 185.1). QLD — greater of contract rate and the Civil Proceedings Act 2011 (Qld) s 59(3) money-order-debt rate, and for "building contracts" the QBCC Act s 67P **penalty rate** (10% + UCA default rate) plus possible offence. SA rates are much lower and fixed.
- **Crown contracts:** SA — from 1 May 2025, Crown contracts > $4m (ex GST) are exempt from deemed liability, adjudication and suspension (reg 7) — a unique SA carve-out with no QLD analogue.
- **Residential work:** SA excludes only the owner-occupier contract itself (s 7(2)(b)). QLD covers domestic building work generally (with the owner-occupier exception in BIF sch 2 / s 61(2) terms).
- **No SA equivalents** of QLD's project bank accounts/trust accounts regime, supporting statements, or claimant-must-be-licensed jurisprudence (*Cant Contracting*-type issues arise under different SA licensing law; the SA Act itself imposes no licensing precondition).
- **Judicial review:** both follow *Probuild/Maxcon* — review for jurisdictional error only; SA additionally has HCA authority directly on its own Act (*Maxcon*).
- **Adjudication response window:** SA — later of 5 bd after receiving the application copy and 2 bd after notice of acceptance (s 20(1)). QLD — 10 bd (standard) / 15 bd (complex) after copy of application + notice of referral, extendable for complex claims.
- **Status note:** SA's Act is the **original 2009 text, never amended** — the 2017 and 2021 reform Bills (Moss Review 2015 and Murray Review 2018 implementations, incl. removing reference dates and domestic building coverage) **lapsed** and had not been re-introduced as at June 2026. Do not assume NSW's 2019 amendments (10-bd schedules, supporting statements, corporations-in-liquidation bar, reference-date removal) apply in SA — none of them do.

3. **Romaldi Full Court citation** — Jade lists the Full Court decision as [2013] SASCFC 124; some commentary refers to two Full Court judgments in the dispute. Verify the precise medium-neutral citation(s) if the case is to be cited in user-facing output.
