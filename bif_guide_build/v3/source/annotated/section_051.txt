[2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 1] SECTION 51 – Principal and subcontractor to be given copy of payment instruction information
[2.1 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 2] A    Legislation
[1 BIFSOPA Heading] 51    Principal and subcontractor to be given copy of payment instruction information
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] This section applies if a head contractor gives a financial institution a payment instruction for the payment of an amount from a trust account for a project bank account. 
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] The head contractor must, as soon as practicable after giving the financial institution the payment instruction, ensure a copy of the information contained in the payment instruction is given to—
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the principal; and 
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] if the payment is to a subcontractor beneficiary—the subcontractor beneficiary. 
[1.1 BIFSOPA Body Text] Maximum penalty—100 penalty units.
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] However, the copy of the information need only include the information prescribed by regulation. 
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] In giving a copy of the information under subsection (2), the head contractor must not give the principal or subcontractor beneficiary information the contractor knows is false or misleading in a material particular. 
[1.1 BIFSOPA Body Text] Maximum penalty—200 penalty units.
[2.1 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 2] B    Commentary
[2.2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 3] 51.1    Power, Obligations, and restrictions for Trustees
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] In Re PBS Building (Qld) Pty Ltd [2024] QSC 108, Brown J held that the effect of s 51A broadens the prohibition on using trust monies to pay debts owed to a creditor of the trustee, regardless of whether the debt was incurred in the trustee’s role or otherwise. In any event, even on the applicants’ proposed construction, the liquidator or administrator would be a creditor of the trustee and precluded from claiming its remuneration, costs and expenses by s 51A(1) of the BIF Act. Brown J disagreed with the interpretation that, in the event of the company’s liquidation, section 60-5 of the Insolvency Practice Schedule grants the administrator or liquidator a statutory indemnity and lien over the company’s trust property to claim remuneration from trust property held by the company and administered by a liquidator or administrator. This argument creates inconsistency with the express terms of the BIF Act, which abrogate a trustee’s right of indemnity, and was not supported by any provisions or extrinsic evidence indicating the reactivation of that right for the benefit of the external administrator.
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] In Re PBS Building (Qld) Pty Ltd [2024] QSC 108, Brown J held that the reference to ‘trustee’ in sections 51C and 51E is not confined to the head building contractor. It would extend to the administrators and liquidators administering the trust. The language does not support the argument that section 51C applies only to head contractors and not to external administrators. Instead, it is meant to prevent a trustee or creditor from arguing that the debt was incurred outside of trust administration. Section 51C(2) specifies that trustees cannot recover costs from the trust funds for administering a project or retention trust.
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] In Re PBS Building (Qld) Pty Ltd [2024] QSC 108, Brown J held that the reference to ‘trustee’ in sections 51C and 51E is not limited to the head building contractor; it extends to administrators and liquidators administering the trust. Section 51E reinforces the prohibition in section 51C against recovering costs from the trust funds for administering a project or retention monies by extending it to anyone employed by the trustee to manage the trust, ensuring that the trustee cannot circumvent the restriction by outsourcing trust administration.
