[2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 1] SECTION 140 – Vexatious claims
[2.1 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 2] A    Legislation
[1 BIFSOPA Heading] 140    Vexatious claims 
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] A person who vexatiously, or without reasonable grounds, gives a notice of claim to another person is liable to pay to a person prejudicially affected by the claim an amount for the damages the person incurs because of the claim. 
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] Without limiting subsection (1), a person gives a notice of a claim without reasonable grounds if the person knows, or ought reasonably to know, that the amount of the claim unreasonably exceeds the amount actually payable to the person. 
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] Damages mentioned in subsection (1) must be decided by a court on an application by the person prejudicially affected.
[2.1 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 2] B    Commentary
[2.2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 3] 141.1    The meaning of “vexatiously or without reasonable cause”
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] It is far from clear what kind of conduct, on the part of the subcontractor, would make the giving of a notice unreasonable or vexatious. See JM Hill & Sons Ltd v London Borough of Camden (1980) 18 BLR 31, per Lawton LJ at p 44. There may be a dozen reasons, all extraneous to the purpose of the BIFSOPA, for wishing to give a notice of claim. But if the person has one good and reasonable ground for giving the notice, then that is sufficient and it is not invalidated by the existence of the dozen reasons. See Hounslow London Borough Council v Twickenham Garden Developments Ltd [1971] Ch 233, per Megarry J (as he then was) at p 267.
