[2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 1] SECTION 91 – Adjudication certificate 
[1 BIFSOPA Heading] 91    Adjudication certificate
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] As soon as practicable after being given a copy of a decision by an adjudicator, but no later than 5 business days after being given the decision, the registrar must give the claimant a certificate (an adjudication certificate) of the decision stating the following matters—
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the name of the claimant; 
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the name of the respondent who is liable to pay the adjudicated amount; 
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the adjudicated amount;
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the date on which payment of the adjudicated amount was required to be paid to the claimant;
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the rate of interest payable on the adjudicated amount; 
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the fees, identified in the decision, that the respondent is to pay;
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] that the certificate is made under this Act.
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] However, the registrar does not have to give the claimant an adjudication certificate if— 
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the respondent is not required to pay an amount under the decision; or 
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the adjudicator decided he or she does not have jurisdiction to decide the application; or
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] the adjudicator decided the adjudication application is frivolous or vexatious.
