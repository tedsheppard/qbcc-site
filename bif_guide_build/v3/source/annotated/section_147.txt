[2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 1] SECTION 147 – Qualified persons 
[2.1 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 2] A    Legislation
[1 BIFSOPA Heading] 147    Qualified persons 
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] A person is a qualified person for the purpose of certifying a notice of claim under section 122 if the person is—
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] an architect registered under the Architects Act 2002; or 
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] a registered professional engineer under the Professional Engineers Act 2002; or
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] a person licensed under the Queensland Building and Construction Commission Act 1991 to carry out or supervise work of the type to which the claim relates; or 
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] a quantity surveyor who is a member of the Australian Institute of Quantity Surveyors; or
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] a person having expert knowledge of the work to which the claim relates and who is accepted in a particular case as a qualified person by the contractor and subcontractor.
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] A person must not give a certificate for a claim that relates to any work if the person— 
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] performed any of the work; or
[1.4 BIFSOPA level 2 (CDI)] has a direct or indirect financial interest in the work. 
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] If a person gives a certificate for a claim in contravention of subsection (2), the claim is of no effect and the relevant subcontractor’s charge does not attach. 
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] A person who gives a certificate for a claim does not have a financial interest in the work only because the person is paid a fee for giving the certificate. 
[1.3 BIFSOPA level 1 (CDI)] A person who gives a certificate for a claim does not incur civil liability by reason only of the giving of the certificate unless the person is convicted of fraud in relation to giving the certificate.
[2.1 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 2] B    Commentary
[2.2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 3] 147.1    General
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] What is required to be certified is that the certifier has examined the claim and in his opinion it is prima facie a claim to proceed pursuant to the provisions of the BIFSOPA (Qld). The qualification and the necessary expertise to entitle a person to make a certificate of this kind will obviously vary with the nature of the work and its extent: Re C W Norris & Co Pty Ltd, Robb Brown Ltd (unreported, QSC, 0813 of 85) at p 5.
[2.2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 3] 147.2    Time limits
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] The additional requirement of certification is something which has to be done within the limited periods permitted by the SCA (now see BIFSOPA (Qld)): Re C W Norris & Co Pty Ltd, Robb Brown Ltd (unreported, QSC, 0813 of 85) at p 4.
[2.2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 3] 147.3    Noncompliance
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] The consequence of a failure to certify a claim of charge in accordance with subs (1) (or semble, subs (2)) is that the claim of charge is invalid under s 10(6) (now see s 122BIFSOPA (Qld)) and should be cancelled under s 21 (now see s 139BIFSOPA (Qld)). Ronnor Pty Ltd v D & R Fabrications Pty Ltd [1983] 2 Qd R 455 applied in Re Australian Lawn & Turf Supplies Pty Ltd [1988] 1 Qd R 5. However, there is nothing in the SCA (now BIFSOPA (Qld)) to prevent a claimant serving, within time, a second set of notices creating a charge without withdrawing the notice of claim of charge: Road Surfaces Group Pty Ltd v Brown [1987] 2 Qd R 792.
[2.2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 3] 147.4    The meaning of “accepted in a particular case”
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] Although this phrase may be intended to refer to the work of the subject of the particular claim or contract or to a dispute arising out of it, it may not extend to dealings or relations merely of a general kind: Ronnor Pty Ltd v D & R Fabrications Pty Ltd [1983] 2 Qd R 455.
[2.2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 3] 147.5    Estoppel through silence
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] There is no duty on a contractor to give notice of a dispute of the claim on the basis that it is not properly certified within the time prescribed, that is, 14 days after the giving of the notice of claim. This is simply because, if the subcontractor did so, then the subcontractor would have had the opportunity to obtain other certification and give a fresh notice. Therefore, an omission to do so will not ground an estoppel: see Ronnor Pty Ltd v D & R Fabrications Pty Ltd [1983] 2 Qd R 455; and Re Australian Lawn & Turf Supplies Pty Ltd [1988] 1 Qd R 5.
[2.2 Com-BIFSOPA Heading 3] 147.6    Subsection (2)
[2.4 Com-BIFSOPA CDI Normal Body Text] A person who is otherwise a qualified person within s 147 has a specific interest within subs (2) if the person has performed any part of the work to which a notice of claim of charge related; even if at the time the person signs the certificate, the person has fully paid for the work performed: Stonebridge v Broadway Interiors & Shopfitters Pty Ltd [1991] 2 Qd R 471.
