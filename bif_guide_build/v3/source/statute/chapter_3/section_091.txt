# Source: BIF Act 2017 — Chapter 3
# Section 91 — Adjudication certificate
(1) As soon as practicable after being given a copy of a decision
by an adjudicator, but no later than 5 business days after being
given the decision, the registrar must give the claimant a
certificate (an adjudication certificate) of the decision stating
the following matters—
(a) the name of the claimant;

(b) the name of the respondent who is liable to pay the
adjudicated amount;
(c) the adjudicated amount;
(d) the date on which payment of the adjudicated amount
was required to be paid to the claimant;
(e) the rate of interest payable on the adjudicated amount;
(f) the fees, identified in the decision, that the respondent is
to pay;
(g) that the certificate is made under this Act.
(2) However, the registrar does not have to give the claimant an
adjudication certificate if—
(a) the respondent is not required to pay an amount under
the decision; or
(b) the adjudicator decided he or she does not have
jurisdiction to decide the application; or
(c) the adjudicator decided the adjudication application is
frivolous or vexatious.
