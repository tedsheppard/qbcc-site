Requirements of an adjudication application

Pay the prescribed fee

An adjudication application must be accompanied by the fee fixed by regulation. Without payment of the fee there is no valid application; the requirement operates alongside the approved form, the timing rules and the obligation to identify the payment claim and schedule. The fee tier is keyed to the size of the progress payment claimed, so the relevant figure is the amount on the payment claim, not the broader contract sum.

Section 79(2)(d) BIF Act — fee requirement

(2) An adjudication application—

…

(d) must be accompanied by the fee prescribed by regulation for the application.

The fee schedule

The prescribed fees are set out in the Building Industry Fairness (Security of Payment) Regulation 2018, by reference to the size of the progress payment claimed. The schedule below is current as at 1 July 2025:

  • Progress payment of no more than $11,345.70 — $60.70.
  • More than $11,345.70 but no more than $56,728.80 — $182.15.
  • More than $56,728.80 but no more than $113,457.60 — $303.60.
  • More than $113,457.60 but no more than $283,644.05 — $425.20.
  • More than $283,644.05 but no more than $567,288.05 — $546.60.
  • More than $567,288.05 but no more than $850,932.15 — $668.05.
  • More than $850,932.15 but no more than $1,236,206.95 — $789.55.
  • More than $1,236,206.95 — the number of fee units equal to 0.0794188% of the progress payment amount, capped at 6,073.80 fee units.

The Regulation gives a worked example for the top tier: an application made on 1 July 2025 for a progress payment of $1,300,000 attracts a fee of $1,032.44 (942.01 fee units).1

Pick the tier from the claim amount

The relevant figure is the amount of the progress payment to which the adjudication application relates — in practice, the amount on the payment claim being adjudicated. A claim for $250,000 falls in tier (d) and attracts a fee of $425.20. A claim for $900,000 falls in tier (g) and attracts a fee of $789.55. For large claims (above approximately $1.236M) the fee scales with the claim amount until it reaches the cap.

Two practical points follow. First, the fee is paid to the QBCC on lodgement: the application is made to the registrar (s 79(1)) and the registrar will not refer the application to an adjudicator under s 79(5) without the prescribed fee accompanying it. Second, fee units are reset annually under the standard Queensland fee-units mechanism, so the current value should be checked before relying on a dollar figure for a tier (h) calculation.

Where the fee fits among the other s 79(2) requirements

Section 79(2) sets out a series of mandatory requirements: approved form (s 79(2)(a)); within time (s 79(2)(b)); identification of the claim and any schedule (s 79(2)(c)); and accompaniment by the prescribed fee (s 79(2)(d)). The Queensland courts have treated the s 79(2) requirements as mandatory in character. In Kangaroo Point Developments MP Property Pty Ltd v RHG Construction Fitout and Maintenance Pty Ltd [2021] QSC 30, Dalton J drew the contrast between the “must” language used in s 79(2)(a) and (c) and the “may” language used elsewhere in the section, holding that “must” denotes a mandatory requirement of the Act.2 Paragraph (d) is drafted in the same mandatory form, so the same reasoning applies: the prescribed fee is not a discretionary addition.

The fee is, however, the most administratively straightforward of the four requirements. Lodgement through the QBCC’s electronic system prompts for payment as part of the lodgement step, so the practical risk is paying the wrong amount, usually by misidentifying the tier, rather than failing to pay altogether.

Practice tips

  • Identify the amount of the progress payment on the payment claim being adjudicated. That figure — not the contract sum, and not the running total of the project — determines the tier.
  • For claims up to $1,236,206.95, the dollar fee can be taken directly from the schedule above. For claims above that figure, calculate 0.0794188% of the claim amount, check it against the 6,073.80 fee unit cap and convert at the current fee-unit value.
  • Pay through the QBCC’s lodgement system at the time of lodgement. Payment cannot be deferred: s 79(2)(d) requires the fee to accompany the application.
  • Re-check the schedule each financial year. The thresholds and dollar figures are indexed and the regulation is republished. The figures above are current as at 1 July 2025.
  • If a claim sits on a threshold (for example $56,728.80 exactly), the words must be read carefully: each tier is “more than” the lower bound and “no more than” the upper bound, so a claim on the threshold falls in the lower tier.
