Requirements of an adjudication application

Pay the prescribed fee

An adjudication application must be accompanied by the fee fixed by regulation. No fee, no valid application — the requirement sits alongside the approved form, the timing rules and the obligation to identify the payment claim and schedule. The fee tier is keyed to the size of the progress payment you are claiming, so the right number to look at is the amount on the payment claim, not the broader contract sum.

Section 79(2)(d) BIF Act — fee requirement

(2) An adjudication application—

…

(d) must be accompanied by the fee prescribed by regulation for the application.

The fee schedule

The prescribed fees sit in the Building Industry Fairness (Security of Payment) Regulation 2018, set by reference to the size of the progress payment claimed. The schedule below is current as at 1 July 2025:

  • Progress payment of no more than $11,345.70 — $60.70.
  • More than $11,345.70 but no more than $56,728.80 — $182.15.
  • More than $56,728.80 but no more than $113,457.60 — $303.60.
  • More than $113,457.60 but no more than $283,644.05 — $425.20.
  • More than $283,644.05 but no more than $567,288.05 — $546.60.
  • More than $567,288.05 but no more than $850,932.15 — $668.05.
  • More than $850,932.15 but no more than $1,236,206.95 — $789.55.
  • More than $1,236,206.95 — the number of fee units equal to 0.0794188% of the progress payment amount, capped at 6,073.80 fee units.

The Regulation gives a worked example for the top tier: an application made on 1 July 2025 for a progress payment of $1,300,000 attracts a fee of $1,032.44 (942.01 fee units).1

Pick the tier from the claim amount

The relevant figure is the amount of the progress payment to which the adjudication application relates — in practice the amount on the payment claim that is being adjudicated. If your claim is for $250,000, you fall in tier (d) and pay $425.20. If your claim is for $900,000, you fall in tier (g) and pay $789.55. For very large claims (above roughly $1.236M) the fee scales with the claim amount until it hits the cap.

Two practical points. First, the fee is paid to the QBCC on lodgement — the application is made to the registrar (s 79(1)) and the registrar will not refer the application to an adjudicator under s 79(5) without the prescribed fee accompanying it. Second, fee units are reset annually under the standard Queensland fee-units mechanism, so check the current value before relying on a dollar figure for a tier (h) calculation.

Where the fee fits among the other s 79(2) requirements

Section 79(2) sets out a stack of mandatory requirements: approved form (s 79(2)(a)); within time (s 79(2)(b)); identify the claim and any schedule (s 79(2)(c)); and accompanied by the prescribed fee (s 79(2)(d)). The Queensland courts have treated the s 79(2) requirements as mandatory in character. In Kangaroo Point Developments MP Property Pty Ltd v RHG Construction Fitout and Maintenance Pty Ltd [2021] QSC 30, Dalton J drew the contrast between the “must” language used in s 79(2)(a) and (c) and the “may” language used elsewhere in the section, holding that “must” denotes a mandatory requirement of the Act.2 Paragraph (d) is drafted in the same mandatory form, so the same logic applies: the prescribed fee is not a discretionary add-on.

That said, the fee is the most administratively straightforward of the four requirements. Lodgement through the QBCC’s electronic system prompts for payment as part of the lodgement step, so the practical risk is paying the wrong amount — usually by misidentifying the tier — rather than forgetting to pay altogether.

What this means on a project

  • Identify the amount of the progress payment on the payment claim being adjudicated. That figure — not the contract sum, not the running total of the project — drives the tier.
  • For claims up to $1,236,206.95, read the dollar fee straight off the schedule above. For claims above that, calculate 0.0794188% of the claim amount, then check it against the 6,073.80 fee unit cap and convert at the current fee-unit value.
  • Pay through the QBCC’s lodgement system at the time you lodge. Don’t plan to pay it later — s 79(2)(d) requires the fee to accompany the application.
  • Re-check the schedule each financial year. The thresholds and dollar figures are indexed and the regulation is republished — the figures above are current as at 1 July 2025.
  • If a claim straddles a threshold (e.g. $56,728.80 exactly), read the words carefully: each tier is “more than” the lower bound and “no more than” the upper bound, so a claim sitting on the threshold falls in the lower tier.
