2. What SopalAI is (and isn't)

SopalAI is a domain-specific research tool. It is engineered to answer questions about Queensland security-of-payment law by:

  • retrieving on-point text from a curated corpus of statutes and Queensland judgments;
  • passing that text to a large language model under tight constraints;
  • composing an answer with mechanically-verified verbatim quotes and footnoted citations to the underlying primary sources.

SopalAI is not:

  • a substitute for a lawyer or for independent legal advice;
  • a court filing system, document management system, or formal record;
  • warranted to be exhaustive of every relevant authority on any topic; or
  • a guarantee that the law as stated in any answer is current, accurate, or applicable to your specific facts.

3. Sources SopalAI relies on

SopalAI's primary sources include:

  • the Building Industry Fairness (Security of Payment) Act 2017 (Qld) and its Regulation;
  • the Queensland Building and Construction Commission Act 1991 and its Regulation;
  • the Acts Interpretation Act 1954 (Qld); and
  • the Queensland line of judgments interpreting those Acts (Queensland Supreme Court, Court of Appeal, and District Court decisions).

Our corpus is a snapshot taken at a particular date and is not updated in real time. New decisions, statutory amendments, or commencement of provisions occurring after the snapshot date will not be reflected in answers.

4. Hallucinations, errors, and accuracy

SopalAI can be wrong

Like every large-language-model–powered system, SopalAI can produce plausible-sounding but incorrect outputs ("hallucinations"). It can mis-state the law, miss relevant authority, mis-apply doctrine to facts, and reach a wrong conclusion. You must independently verify any output before relying on it.

SopalAI is engineered with several mechanisms aimed at reducing — but not eliminating — these risks:

  • quotes are mechanically extracted verbatim from the underlying chunks rather than being generated by the language model, so quoted passages match their source text;
  • citation markers point to specific paragraphs of judgments or sections of statutes that you can open and read in full;
  • a knowledge-augmented planner names provisions and authorities, and a separate retrieval layer resolves those names against the indexed corpus before any answer is composed.

Notwithstanding these design measures:

  • no machine-learning system is free from error;
  • retrieval can miss relevant material that exists in the corpus;
  • the corpus itself may not contain every relevant authority;
  • the language model can mis-characterise on-point material or apply it to a question in a way a careful lawyer would not; and
  • citation markers may, in edge cases, be applied to text that does not fully support the cited proposition.

You agree that the existence of these limitations is a known feature of large-language-model systems, that you are using SopalAI on this basis, and that no representation or warranty is made by Sopal that any output is correct, complete, or current.
