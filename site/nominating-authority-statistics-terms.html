Nominating Authority Statistics Terms

Terms applying to Sopal’s nominating authority and adjudicator-affiliation statistics

Last updated: 12 July 2026

These terms form part of Sopal’s Terms of Use and Account Terms. By accessing or using the Nominating Authority Statistics, you agree to these terms.

1. Scope and incorporation

These Nominating Authority Statistics Terms apply to the page at sopal.com.au/nominating-authorities, related charts, tables, exports, descriptions and any other Sopal feature presenting statistics grouped by a nominating authority or by an adjudicator’s association with a nominating authority (the Statistics).

These terms apply in addition to Sopal’s Terms of Use, Account Terms, Subscription Terms and Privacy Policy. If there is an inconsistency concerning the Statistics, these terms prevail to the extent of the inconsistency.

2. Nature of the data

The Statistics are derived from published Queensland adjudication decisions and associated extracted data. The underlying decisions were prepared for particular disputes and may contain omissions, errors, inconsistent terminology or incomplete information.

Before December 2014, Queensland adjudication applications were generally received and referred by authorised nominating authorities under the former statutory scheme. From December 2014, Queensland applications have instead been received and referred through the Queensland Adjudication Registry. Accordingly, a post-December 2014 decision grouped under an ANA does not mean that the ANA received, referred, selected, controlled or influenced the application or its outcome.

3. Attribution and inference

Sopal may identify an ANA affiliation from the text of a published decision or infer an affiliation from the adjudicator’s documented affiliations in other decisions. Automated keyword extraction, artificial intelligence and other analytical processes may be used.

Affiliations may be incomplete, outdated, non-exclusive or incorrect. An adjudicator may be associated with more than one ANA, may change affiliations over time, or may act independently of an ANA. A grouping is an analytical classification only and is not a statement that the ANA appointed, employed, supervised or was legally responsible for the adjudicator in the relevant matter.

4. Statistical limitations

The Statistics are descriptive and approximate. They may be affected by differences in claim value, dispute type, legal issues, complexity, documentation, parties, time period, statutory regime, sample size and the availability or quality of published data.

  • Averages may exclude decisions for which usable data was unavailable.
  • Percentages and amounts may be rounded, capped or calculated using stated methodology.
  • Small samples may be volatile and should not be treated as representative.
  • Historical outcomes do not predict any future adjudication or appointment.

5. No inference about quality, fairness or causation

The Statistics do not establish or imply that an ANA or adjudicator is favourable or unfavourable to any class of party, biased, unfair, competent or incompetent. They do not measure the quality of reasons, procedural fairness, legal correctness or the suitability of an ANA or adjudicator for a particular dispute.

No causal inference should be drawn between an ANA affiliation and an adjudication outcome. Users must not describe or reproduce the Statistics in a manner that suggests causation, endorsement, misconduct or responsibility that the data does not establish.

6. General information only

The Statistics are provided for general information and research only. They are not legal, financial or professional advice and are not a recommendation to select or avoid an ANA, adjudicator or other service provider. You should independently verify material information and obtain professional advice appropriate to your circumstances.
