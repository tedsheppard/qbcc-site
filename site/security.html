Security at Sopal

Sopal handles construction-law documents for Australian firms. This page sets out, in plain terms, how your documents and data are protected — including exactly what our AI providers can and cannot do with your content.

Your documents

  • Encryption in transit. All traffic is served over TLS (HTTPS). There are no unencrypted endpoints.
  • Encryption at rest. Uploaded documents are stored in Google Cloud Storage, encrypted at rest with AES-256. Extracted document text held in Sopal's own database is additionally encrypted at the application layer, so database files never contain your documents in plaintext.
  • No permanent links. Documents are only ever served through short-lived signed URLs (minutes, not days) or authenticated streams. There are no public object URLs.
  • Tenant isolation. Every document, extracted page, category tag and search index entry is bound to your firm's workspace. Access is resolved from your authenticated membership on every request — a request can never name another firm's data.
  • Hard deletion. Deleting a document permanently removes the stored file, extracted text, category tags and search index entries. Offboarding a firm purges everything the firm uploaded; only the audit record of the purge is retained.
  • Backups. Metadata is backed up nightly (encrypted content included) with a rolling retention window, and stored objects are versioned to protect against accidental deletion.

Access controls & auditability

  • Firm workspaces with roles. Admins manage members and deletions; members upload and search. Invites are token-based and expire after 14 days.
  • Audit log. Every upload, document view, search, deletion and membership change is recorded (who, what, when, from which IP) and is available to your firm's admins.
  • Passwords are stored as bcrypt hashes. Sessions use signed, audience-scoped tokens.

AI data handling

Sopal uses Anthropic's Claude models to categorise documents and power research features. Under Anthropic's commercial API terms:

  • Your content is never used to train models.
  • Retention is limited. API inputs and outputs are retained by Anthropic for up to 30 days for abuse monitoring, then deleted.
  • Zero-data-retention processing is on our roadmap as firm volume grows.

Documents are sent for categorisation once at ingestion (and only again if you re-run categorisation). Search runs entirely on Sopal's infrastructure — your queries and library are not sent to any AI provider.
