Authorised Nominating Authorities

Compare the bodies that referred adjudication applications to adjudicators under the Building and Construction Industry Payments Act 2004 (Qld) — decision volumes, claim sizes, award rates and fee allocations, by authority and by year.

Methodology & Disclaimer

The statistics on this page are approximate. Each decision's referring authority is coded automatically from the text of the published decision document, using keyword extraction and AI extraction. Where a decision document does not name the authority, attribution is inferred from the adjudicator's documented ANA affiliations in other decisions; inferred attributions are approximate. Coding errors and gaps are possible, and decisions that cannot be attributed either way are excluded from the per-authority statistics below.

Authorised Nominating Authorities were abolished for new Queensland adjudication applications from December 2014, when the BCIPA amendments moved referrals to the Adjudication Registry operated by the QBCC. Referrals since then are made by the Registry to adjudicators whose matters an ANA administers, so later decisions are attributed to an ANA where the document names one or the adjudicator's ANA is known. Statistics are provided for general information only, are not legal advice, and say nothing about the quality or fairness of any authority, adjudicator, or decision. Data limitations are described in our Insights terms.
